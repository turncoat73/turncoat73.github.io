Back to My Roots

Well, I always thought I was a decent writer, given that I've had to do a lot of it throughout school and career.

But now, I'm staring at a fairly blank HTML page wondering what to say.

For now, I'll just try and anticipate a few questions and answer them I guess.

Why am I doing this? Why not? I spent a bunch of time (and money) last year learning HTML, CSS, Python, and a little Javascript. I wanted to try and use it before I forget it. Crazy how much I've forgotten already Looking at the current layout of this site, one could be easily forgiven for thinking "helloooo 1995". Fair enough. I get it. It will be improved as I go along.

Why spend time learning these programming languages? I've got a decent grasp on Python already. I wanted to get some sort of formal certification, so I did Udacity's Introduction to Programming class. It was no joke. It got a little frustrating at times. When it got frustrating my tendency was to keep grinding. Big mistake. It's important to walk away, take a break, come back to it. I should have engaged people on the forums a whole lot more.

What will this site be about? I do a lot of quasi-techie projects on my time and my dime. If I think something's worthwhile, I'll post it. Given that I'm using GitHub to host this site, it should be used for this purpose. If there's code involved, I'll share the code. I will avoid writing narcicisstic tomes imposing my views of how the world oughtta be. If readers want that, then social media is what you're looking for. However, I am somewhat philanthropic towards local efforts to improve the literacy rate in the county in which I reside, so from time to time I will probably bring that up.

What's with the little saying? I'm right, aren't I? It is pretty loud and in your face wherever one goes these days. I'm not one of the loud ones at all. So why not try and build a website to say what I gotta say.
